Early Life

Thomas Alva Edison was born on February 11, 1847, in Milan, Ohio. He was the youngest of seven children. Edison was mostly self-educated and showed a strong interest in technology from a young age.

Famous Inventions

Edison held over 1,000 patents. His most notable inventions include the phonograph, the electric light bulb, and the motion picture camera. His work revolutionized the modern world.

Legacy

Thomas Edison died on October 18, 1931. He left behind a legacy of innovation and entrepreneurship. He is remembered as one of the greatest inventors in history.
